Cut Fill with Regions

      • Basic steps
      • Related topics

　　Calculates the volume and area of the cut and fill. 　　For example, when you want to flat a rugged region, you can perform the Cut Fill with Region to obtain the fill area, the cut area, the volume of cut and fill through specifying the rugged area and the elevation of result region. About the theory and method of Cut Fill, please refer to Cut Fill.

Basic steps

  1. In the toolbox, click “Raster Analysis” > “Surface Analysis” > “Terrain Calculate” > “Cut Fill with Regions” to open the “Cut Fill with Regions” dialog box.
  2. Specify a dataset for the calculation.
  3. Select a line or region dataset as the reference data. To a region object, it can participate in the operation directly as a specified plane. To a line object, a buffer analysis will be done first, and the result will take part in the calculation as a specified plane.
  4. Append Height: Result elevation of cut or fill.

  5. Buffer settings: It is available for line objects.

    • Buffer Type: Select a buffer type as your needs. Two types are provided: Flat which means there are bevels at line ends and nodes, and Round which means both the ends and nodes are round.
    • Buffer Radius: Determines the buffer range by buffer radius, and creates buffer for line objects to form the area to be cut and filled.

  6. Set a name for the result dataset and specify a datasource to save it, then click “Run” image button to perform the operation. After this operation is done, it will display the cut and fill result in the output window, including the Fill Volume, Cut Volume, Fill Area, Cut Area, and Left Area five contents.
